## Enhancing Robotic Performance with AI

DARPA's Machine Common Sense (MCS) program  is at the forefront  of advancing the  common sense capabilities of AI systems. Researchers involved in this program have made  significant strides in improving robotic system performance[^3]. By leveraging cutting-edge AI hardware, they  are  equipping robots with a greater understanding of the world, enabling them to make more informed decisions  and interact seamlessly  with their surroundings. This development marks a crucial milestone in the  integration  of AI and robotics.

## The AI Hardware Program: A Catalyst for Innovation

Recognizing the transformative potential of AI hardware, MIT has launched the AI  Hardware Program in collaboration with industry giants such as Amazon, Analog Devices, ASML,  NTT Research, and TSMC[^4]. This program  aims to drive innovation  in next-generation AI hardware, including hardware specifically designed  for robotics. The partnership  between academia and industry is essential in pushing the boundaries  of  what AI-powered robots  can achieve. Through this program, we can expect even more cutting-edge AI hardware solutions  to  emerge fueling the rise of intelligent machines.
